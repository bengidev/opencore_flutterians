# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

adaptive

## Users

Primary audience is developers using Android or iOS who want to plan, draft, review, and ship from a phone without bouncing between chat apps, notes, browsers, and IDE tabs. They are in focused work sessions—commuting, away from desk, or between meetings—and need AI that stays inside a permissioned workspace with local context intact.

## Product Purpose

OpenCore Flutterians is a cross-platform mobile workspace that integrates frontier AI models into one task-oriented surface. Success means a developer can finish real work in a single app—drafting, refactoring, research, interface decisions—while getting fast, trustworthy model turns without losing thread or leaking context across tools. The product exists to replace fragmented mobile workflows with **perfection fusion**: the human stays in the loop while AI handles reasoning and generation on-device, under the user's keys and rules.

## Positioning

The mobile command center where developers work with AI in context—not another chat tab.

## Brand Personality

Precise, focused, technical. The interface should feel industrial and warm at once: high signal, no decorative noise, typography and spacing doing the hierarchy work. Visual language draws from Nothing-inspired craft (monochrome canvas, typographic structure, tactile controls) with minimal 6pt rounded rectangles on interactive elements—not bubbly consumer chrome.

## Anti-references

- Generic chat-bubble UIs and ChatGPT-clone layouts that treat conversation as the product instead of the task.
- SaaS landing-page aesthetics: cream cards, gradient heroes, eyebrow kickers, ghost-card shadows.
- Over-rounded, bubbly consumer apps where every control is a pill and hierarchy is flattened into identical tiles.

## Design Principles

1. **Context is the feature.** Every screen should make it obvious what workspace, thread, or artifact the user is acting on. Switching apps should never feel like the cheaper path.
2. **Subtract, don't add.** Controls earn their pixels. Prefer spacing and type scale over borders, cards, and ornament.
3. **Developer-native density.** Information-rich when the task demands it; familiar affordances over novelty. The tool should disappear into the flow.
4. **Tactile precision.** Interactive elements use restrained geometry (6pt radius), clear press feedback, and fast state transitions—responsive without decorative motion.
5. **Trust by default.** Local-aware posture (keys, rules, pairing) is part of the experience story, not a settings footnote.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Follow platform defaults (iOS HIG, Material 3) for touch targets, Dynamic Type / text scaling, and contrast. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` for animations. No additional WCAG level mandated beyond standard platform accessibility expectations.
